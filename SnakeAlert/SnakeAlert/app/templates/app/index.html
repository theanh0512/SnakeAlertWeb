Current Situation

Snakebite is recognized as a crucial public health issue by World Health Organization from 2009. Envenoming resulting from snakebite is a fatal injury causing more than 100,000 deaths and 400,000 amputations annually out of 4.5-5.5 million incidents globally. Countries like Africa, Australia, Bangladesh, India and Sri Lanka are especially afflicted by the snakebite problem due to their geographical positions and climatic conditions. In rural area like Bangladesh, snakebite is a major cause of mortality and morbidity, it significantly impacts economies through treatment related expenditure and productivity.
In Australia where snakebites are also common, timely rescue of victims and adequate training of medical officers in identifying snake venom and administering anti-venom allow the death toll to be effectively reduced. By comparison, in Bangladesh, resource scarcity and lack of medical support causes treatment to arrive at a later stage, resulting in venom to penetrate body organs and create permanent damage, sometimes causing deaths.
With the help of adequate cell phone coverage, we propose to implement a comprehensive mobile-based system to facilitate emergent first aid service and provide all-rounded communication service to support on-demand treatment and care in rural areas. So life-saving service are made available with technology mediated support and explored further on as a general public health communication solution.
